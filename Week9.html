Close reading: In "Digital Inequalities in the age of artificial intelligence and big data", Lutz explores the unequal distribution of benefits that come with modern technologies in the context of artificial intelligence and big data. Lutz highlights the importance of the unequal distribution by stating that "Access to the internet" is an "essential condition" in order to distribute the benefits that come with the digital age. In the text Lutz also notes that "Digital Literacy" plays an essential role in shaping the digital divide. People who lack the skills to properly use modern technology will not reap the benefits of it either. The author emphasises the importance of having digital literacy with regards to AI and big data. Without the skills to properly analyse and interpret data, individuals face a disadvantage in accessing the opportunities these technologies provide. Furthermore, Lutz raises concerns with regards to biases and discriminatory outcomes that may unfold with AI and big data. The text states that AI and big data can create "new forms of discrimination and inequality" if the AI reproduces existing biases and discriminations. The algorithms used in AI collect data for analyses. This data can be influenced by societal biases. The author emphasises the need for transparency in these AI systems and the need for developers to to prioritize fairness and consider ethical concerns in their design.

Reference: Lutz, C. (2019). Digital Inequalities in the Age of Artificial Intelligence and Big Data. p. 141 - 147
